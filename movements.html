Movements That Started Online

Digital technology can help shape public opinion; anyone can start a political blog or upload a video of police abuse. Ordinary folks can become citizen journalists in their own right, they can create their own stories or rebroadcast news stories. This works on a global scale so it’s easy to see why some might say it could be considered a mainstream event. But movements can start small, a simple hashtag or simple social media post can become a global phenomena. Let’s take a look at a couple of examples of global movements that were started online.

Black Lives Matter

Black Lives Matter started in 2013, starting as a social media post using #BlackLivesMatter after George Zimmerman was acquitted for killing Trayvon Martin, an unarmed Black teenager. The movement protests police brutality and racially motivated crimes against Black people. They gained more traction when they organized protests during the 2016 election and expanded the movement to a national network. Even more recently, in 2020, Black Lives Matter became one of the largest movements in the U.S. when people came out en masse to protest the killing of George Floyd, along with several other innocent Black people, at the hands of police. Black Lives Matter has also experienced a globalization of their cause.
